Description

Lasagna is an Italian classic for the ages. Originating from Naples, Italy in the Middle Ages. Most people you know will have childhood memories of a homecooked lasagna. Personally, it brings me memories of my Mum making us Lasagna on our birthday dinner nights.

The rich tomato sauce breaking up the crisp pasta layers, it's a dish that anybody should have at least once in their life. Without further ado, how to make a lasagna.

Ingredients:

  • Meat: Italian sausage and lean ground beef. (Use mushrooms for a vegetarian alternate)
  • Aromatics: An onion and two cloves of garlic to cook with the meat.
  • Tomatoes: A can of crushed tomatoes, two cans of tomato sauce and two cans of tomato paste. For the sauce.
  • Sweetness: At least two tablespoons of white sugar to balance the tomatoes.
  • Seasonings: Fresh parsley, dried basil, salt, italian herbs, fennel seeds and black pepper. For a bit of flavour
  • Pasta: Lasagna sheets, fresh or store bought.
  • Cheese: Parmesan, mozzarella and ricotta cheese.
  • Egg: A single egg.

Steps:

  1. Cook off the aromatics before adding your meat of choice.
  2. Add the tomato elements and simmer them off for 10 minutes. Add the sugar to lower the tartness of the tomatoes if needed.
  3. Season the sauce to taste.
  4. Let the sauce simmer whilst preparing the lasagna sheets.
  5. Mix the egg with the ricotta. (This will allow the ricotta to hold it's shape better)
  6. Before layering the lasagna, cover the base of the lasagna dish with a small amount of sauce. (Will prevent the lasagna sticking to the base)
  7. Begin layering the lasagna. The order being pasta sheet then meat sauce then ricotta then some parmessan and mozzarella slices.
  8. Repeat the layering until a small amount of meat sauce and ricotta remains.
  9. For the final layer place meat sauce then top with ricotta. Then cover the top with the parmesan and mozzarella, this will give you that golden brown top.
  10. Cover the finished lasagna with alfoil wrap.
  11. Place the covered lasagna into a 190°C preheated oven. Cook for a total of 50 minutes, 25 minutes covered and 25 minutes uncovered. (The top of the lasagna should be golden brown at this point, cook longer uncovered if need for colour)
  12. After pulling the lasagna from the oven, allow it to sit for 15 minutes to cool. (If you don't slices will not hold their shape)
  13. Serve the lasagna. (Accompanying with some garlic bread is common)
